Post-Silicon Steps in VLSI Design

Understanding the Process After Chip Fabrication

1. Wafer Testing

Each fabricated wafer undergoes electrical testing to identify functional and defective dies before packaging. This ensures that only fully functional chips are sent for the next stages.

2. Packaging

The verified dies are packaged to protect the chip and provide electrical connections to external systems. The packaging ensures thermal management and mechanical protection of the chip.

3. Post-Silicon Validation

Chips are tested in real hardware environments to validate performance, functionality, and reliability. These tests help ensure that the chip performs as expected in real-world conditions.

4. Failure Analysis

If defects are detected during validation, detailed failure analysis is performed to understand the root causes and to improve future manufacturing processes.

5. Product Qualification

Final chips undergo rigorous reliability and stress testing to confirm they can endure operational conditions over time before they are released to the market for commercial use.
